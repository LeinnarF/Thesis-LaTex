\section{Results}

This section presents the findings of the study across three areas:
(1) the comparative benchmarking of seven metaheuristic algorithms for
Traveling Salesman Problem (TSP) route optimization, (2) the implemented
system features of \textit{Lakad} as the primary output of the development process,
and (3) the evaluation results of system acceptability and software quality.

\subsection{Route Optimization Selection}

Seven metaheuristic algorithms were benchmarked against four standard
TSPLIB instances to determine the most suitable route optimization
algorithm for deployment in \textit{Lakad}. Performance was measured across two
criteria: solution quality, expressed as average relative error (\%)
against known optimal solutions, and runtime efficiency, expressed in
seconds.

As shown in Table~\ref{tab:acc}, the Genetic Algorithm (GA) recorded the
lowest average relative error across all instances at 0.10\%, indicating
the highest solution quality among the seven algorithms. Simulated
Annealing (SA) and Ant Colony Optimization (ACO) followed with average
errors of 1.59\% and 2.50\%, respectively. The remaining four algorithms
— EHO (11.27\%), PSO (23.77\%), HSFFPKO (61.11\%), and GWO (91.80\%) —
produced substantially higher errors, with degradation becoming more
pronounced on the larger eil51 and berlin52 instances due to their
stochastic nature.

Table~\ref{tab:runtime} shows that EHO recorded the fastest runtimes
across all instances, completing the largest test case (berlin52) in under
0.5 seconds. SA and GWO maintained consistently short runtimes, each
completing all instances in under 2 seconds. By contrast, GA's runtime
scaled exponentially with problem size, requiring 196.59 seconds on the
berlin52 instance. ACO showed moderate to high runtimes, increasing from
2.51 seconds on burma14 to 29.23 seconds on berlin52.
 
 
To resolve the trade-off between solution quality and runtime, the Simple
Additive Weighting (SAW) method was applied, assigning equal weights
($w_1 = w_2 = 0.5$) to both normalized criteria. Table~\ref{tab:rank}
presents the resulting SAW scores and final rankings.

As shown in Table~\ref{tab:rank}, Simulated Annealing (SA) achieved the
lowest SAW scores across all four instances, ranking 1st overall.
GA, despite its superior accuracy, was penalized by its exponential
runtime growth, placing 6th. EHO ranked 2nd due to its speed advantage,
though its accuracy degraded significantly on larger instances.
Consequently, SA was selected as the route optimization algorithm
for Lakad.

\subsection{The Developed System}

The Lakad application was successfully implemented with six features
that collectively address the research objectives of personalized
itinerary generation, route optimization, and tourist destination
promotion in Bulacan. All tourist spot data integrated into the system
was validated and approved by the Provincial History, Arts, Culture,
and Tourism Office (PHACTO).
