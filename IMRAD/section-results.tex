\section{Results and Discussion}

This section presents the results of the study, focusing on the development of a mobile itinerary generator and optimizer for tourist destinations in the province of Bulacan. The results follow a comparative analysis between selected algorithms to solve the Traveling Salesman Problem (TSP). It includes the discussion about the developed system alongside the features available with the application. The reliability and quality of the application is also measured under user acceptability, and software quality assessments. The results of the study provides insights into how modern planning methods affect the tourism sector. It enables policy makers to create more suitable methods of promotion and explore which aspect of the sector can be changed to improve the quality of tourism and provide a better experience for the tourists.

\subsection{Route Optimization Algorithm Selection}
This section shows how the selected metaheuristic algorithms perform when solving TSP. The burma14, bays29, eil51, and berlin52 instances from TSPLIB library were used to test the algorithms. The study aimed to determine which route optimization algorithm would best serve Lakad by evaluating its ability to minimize travel distance and runtime efficiency. The researchers executed each algorithm 10 times to observe its stochastic nature and record the average results for analysis.

Each algorithm has their specific parameters to ensure fair comparison. The Genetic Algorithm (GA) used a population size of 100, tournament selection size of 5, Order Crossover, and 2-opt mutation rate of 0.01. The algorithm operated for 50 generations while maintaining the top 10\% of solution through elitism. Simulated Annealing (SA) was configured with an initial and final temperatures of $T_{start}=1000$ and $T_{end}=1$, a cooling rate of $0.999$, and $n \times 1.5$ iterations per temperature level. Ant Colony Optimization (ACO) deployed 50 ants over 200 epochs, with pheromone factor $\alpha = 1.0$, heuristic information importance $\beta = 5.0$, evaporation rate $\rho = 0.1$, and pheromone deposit factor $q = 100$. Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO) used 30 particles running for 500 iterations, with inertia weight $w = 0.7$, cognitive coefficient $c_1 = 1.4$, social coefficient $c_2 = 1.4$, maximum velocity $v_{max} = 80$, and incorporated 2-opt local search for 40 iterations. The Elephant Herding Optimization (EHO) algorithm had a population of 50 elephants divided into 5 clans, ran for 500 iterations with clan update and separating operators. Grey Wolf Optimization (GWO) was configured with a pack size of 15 wolves and ran for 500 iterations, with linearly decreasing parameter $a$ from 2.0 to 0.0, employing random-key encoding to map continuous values to permutations. Lastly, the Hybrid Pied Kingfisher Optimizer used a population of 50 with 15\% scout birds and 8 foraging flock and ran for 500 iterations of its search within the space bound of [0, 20] for each dimension. The researchers executed the algorithms 30 times for each TSP instance to establish a statistical validity, which resulted in average performance metrics that they used for their analysis. The parameter settings were selected based on preliminary experiments and studied literature recommendations.

Given the results, the Simulated Annealing (SA) was the selected route optimization for Lakad. It provides a good trade-off between solution quality and runtime efficiency, ensuring that users receive optimized itineraries in a reasonable time frame, enhancing the overall user experience of the Lakad application.

\subsection{The Developed System}
The developed system, Lakad, was developed as both a place explorer and itinerary planner with route optimization capabilities.  It allows tourists to search tourist spots within Bulacan and plan their itineraries by generating one for them and gives out navigation instructions for travel. Lakad has five key features namely; Tourist Spot Exploration which includes Tourist Spot Information, and Reviews and Ratings; the Personalized Itinerary Generation feature which contains the Generated Itinerary; the Itinerary Optimization which utilizes the selected algorithm for optimization; the Itinerary Management feature that lets users manage itineraries, including an option to create more manually or automatically; and the Itinerary Navigation feature which serve as the guide for traversing given itinerary. Alongside the core features, the application also has an admin mode for a more efficient management of the application's content by the administrators.
